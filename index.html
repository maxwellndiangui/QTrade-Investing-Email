The great home hunt: why wait?
According to the study, 64% of Canadian home seekers aim for detached homes. But why not consider other options? Starting your homeownership journey with a condo, semi-detached house, or townhouse could be your opportunity to get into the market sooner. And fixer-uppers are still a viable option for those who enjoy a project. The housing market is full of possibilities waiting for you to explore.

Remember, your first home doesn't have to be your forever home. Consider climbing that property ladder with what's within reach now.

Supercharging your down payment: dig into every pocket
Now, let's talk about that down payment. Our study showed personal savings top the list of sources at 49%. After that, 28% of home buyers plan to use a Tax-Free Savings Account (TFSA), and 26% will sell other assets. Additionally, one in five are tapping into investments or using the First Home Savings Account (FHSA), especially younger buyers (18-34) and those expecting to put down less than 20%. A further 14% will source funds from their Registered Retirement Savings Plan (RRSP) through the Home Buyers Plan.  

We've done the math on how you can save and take advantage of the different programs available. 

The buddy system: could co-ownership be your way in?
Here's something else we found: 68% of buyers are teaming up with partners or spouses, while 26% are taking the plunge solo. But there's another option gaining popularity: buying with a family member or even a friend. This strategy can help you share the financial burden and get into your own place faster, not to mention the potential for splitting bills. 

However, as we all know, mixing money with relationships can get...well, complicated. For a deep dive, check out our latest article and consider thinking outside the single-owner box to make your home ownership dream a reality sooner.

Here's to turning those home ownership from a far-off dream to a "we did it!"
